repair problem

An HVAC system is a heating and cooling system that provides thermal comfort and indoor air quality for a building or home. It includes components such as a furnace, air conditioner, ductwork, thermostat, and air filter.

It's recommended to change your air filter every 1-3 months, depending on usage and the type of filter. A dirty air filter can reduce airflow and decrease the efficiency of your HVAC system.

The ideal temperature setting for your thermostat depends on personal preference, the time of year, and the climate in your area. However, a general rule of thumb is to set your thermostat to 68-72 degrees Fahrenheit during the winter and 72-78 degrees Fahrenheit during the summer.

It's recommended to have your HVAC system serviced by a licensed contractor once a year, preferably in the spring or fall. Regular maintenance can help improve the efficiency and lifespan of your system.

Some signs that your HVAC system may need repair include reduced airflow, strange noises, uneven heating or cooling, high humidity levels, and increased energy bills.

You can improve the efficiency of your HVAC system by regularly changing the air filter, sealing air leaks in your home, installing a programmable thermostat, and scheduling regular maintenance with a licensed contractor.

Common HVAC system problems include dirty air filters, refrigerant leaks, malfunctioning thermostats, faulty capacitors or motors, and clogged condensate drains.
